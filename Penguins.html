Palmer Penguins dataset

The Palmer Penguins dataset is a well-known dataset in the R programming language, consisting of measurements of different penguin species that were collected from three islands in the Palmer Archipelago, Antarctica. The dataset is included in the palmerpenguins package in R, which can be easily loaded using the relevant commands.

The dataset was created by Dr. Kristen Gorman and the Palmer Station Long Term Ecological Research (LTER) team. It has become a popular tool for teaching data analysis and visualization in R. The dataset contains 344 observations of penguin species, including Adélie, Chinstrap, and Gentoo, along with 8 variables such as bill length, bill depth, flipper length, body mass, island, sex, and year.

Objective

  1. Is there a relationship between bill length, body mass and penguin species?
  2. Predicting penguin species with multiple linear regression.
  3. Determine how accurate is the prediction of penguin species with KNN.

3. Determine how accurate is the prediction of penguin species with KNN.

set.seed(123)
train_idx <- sample(nrow(penguins_clean), size = round(nrow(penguins_clean) * 0.8), replace = FALSE)
train_data <- penguins_clean[train_idx, ]
test_data <- penguins_clean[-train_idx, ]

k <- 5
predicted_species <- knn(train_data[, c("bill_length_mm", "bill_depth_mm", "flipper_length_mm")],
                         test_data[, c("bill_length_mm", "bill_depth_mm", "flipper_length_mm")],
                         train_data$species, k)

accuracy <- sum(predicted_species == test_data$species) / nrow(test_data)
cat("Accuracy: ", accuracy)
## Accuracy:  0.9701493

The KNN classification predicted the species of penguins based on their bill length, bill depth, and flipper length. It achieved an accuracy of 97% in predicting the correct species using a training and testing set.
